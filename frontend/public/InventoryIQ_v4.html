Calculations
EOQ, Safety Stock, and Reorder Point for all SKUs
Safety Stock
SS = Z × σd × √L
Z = service level factor · σ = demand std dev · L = lead time
Reorder Point
ROP = (d × L) + SS
d = avg daily demand · L = lead time · SS = safety stock
Economic Order Qty
EOQ = √(2DS / H)
D = annual demand · S = order cost · H = holding cost/unit/yr

Supplier Model
Supplier reliability scores, delay probability, and uncertainty-adjusted safety stock
Why Supplier Modeling Matters
Standard safety stock formulas assume orders always arrive on time. In reality, supplier delays amplify stockout risk. This module computes supplier-adjusted safety stock: SS_adj = SS_base × (1 + delay_probability × impact_factor). A supplier with 30% delay probability effectively requires ~30% higher safety stock to maintain the same service level.

Daily Sales Entry
Log actual sales → auto-updates history → triggers model retraining → adjusts decisions
How Auto-Learning Works
Every time you enter today's actual sales: (1) Demand history is updated. (2) If the SKU has 7+ new data points since last training, the ML model automatically retrains. (3) The Decision Engine applies adaptive feedback — if stockouts occurred, safety stock increases; if overstock persists, EOQ decreases. (4) Service level targets adjust per ABC class (A=99%, B=95%, C=90%).

ltstddev: lead time standard deviation
moq: minimum order quantity
shortage: shortage penalty per unit
